To Bear: (2017)

To Bear was my Junior Year Spring Intermediate Animation final where I spent a semester working on from pre-production to post-production. I was curious about the 2D animation style that were made by 3D animation softwares and decided to try it with this project. I created 2D painterly textures in Photoshop and added them as 2D shaders in the Maya Software. The application of both 2D and 3D softwares allowed me to create the 3D lighting atmosphere with a sense of space while having the 2D textures. For this film I also experimented when still shots and minimal changes in camera angles. Originally I wanted it to be one camera angle, but I had to adjust to better convey the story. The softwares I used were Adobe Photoshop, Autodesk Maya, Adobe After Effects, Adobe Premiere and Protools.

Dog Days: (2016)

Dog Days was my Junior Year Fall Intermediate Animation final where I spent a semester working on it from pre-production to post-production. My love for both 2D and 3D computer animation inspired me to combine the two animation techniques. The story was based on a real-life story that I hope to portray my love for dogs and hopefully other audiences can relate. The softwares I used were Autodesk Maya, Adobe After Effects, Adobe Premiere, Adobe Animate, Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop.
